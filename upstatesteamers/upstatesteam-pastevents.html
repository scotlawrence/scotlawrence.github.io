The Upstate Steamers began in 2002, when Ron Brown and Chuck Walters first hatched the idea..as Ron recalls:

"Chuck and I were talking about how nice it would be to have a monthly steamup to attend in the upstate region. We decided to start an informal group and see how many other fellows might be interested. There was instant acceptance, and we had little or no problem filling the calendar every year after that."

-Ron Brown.
 

New members quickly came onboard! and soon four railroad owners were regular hosts:

Chuck Walters had his large raised track in Oswego, NY available for steamup gatherings,
Tom Bowdler had his Garden railroad in Brockport, Roger Caiazza also has a Garden Railroad equipped for live steam near Syracuse, and Ron Brown's well known track at "Paradise East" rounded out the early tracks ..the guys decided to get together once a month, or whenever they could, to have steamups! and so the Upstate steamers club began.

Ron & Marie Brown, publishers of Steam in the Garden magazine, had a large summer 
steamup every August, one of the largest annual steamups in the East.

In 2003 Don Sauer finished his "Don's place to run trains" railroad, and Don and Marion
hosted annual steamups every year between 2003 and 2009.

A calendar first appeared on-line for 2004:


(above is a screenshot from Chuck's webpage (no longer on-line) )


Every year new members have joined the group, and the club's activities continue to expand.
Now, not only do we have steamups at member's home railroads, but the club also does
steamups and live steam demonstrations at public train shows and other public events, 
(thanks to Tom Bowdler and his portable steam track.)

Come out and join us! 
visitors are always welcome.
