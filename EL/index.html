The Saga of SDP45 6634

Wisconsin Central SDP45 6634 anyone know where she is? (EL 3656)
She was auctioned off by CP on July 11, 2002
museum at Spooner? http://www.railfan.net/lists/erielack-digest/200207/msg00269.html (it is now believed this never happened)
or Rail World Inc.? (AARX) (Ed Burkhardt) http://www.railfan.net/lists/erielack-digest/200207/msg00328.html

Update, December 2008.
Im still looking for Wisconsin Central 6634! ;)
several differing reports of her status/demise are floating about. there are SIX conflicting theorys!

1. museum at Spooner? http://www.railfan.net/lists/erielack-digest/200207/msg00269.html (it is now believed this never happened)
2. Rail World Inc.? (AARX) (Ed Burkhardt) http://www.railfan.net/lists/erielack-digest/200207/msg00328.html
3. Was perhaps Indiana Harbor Belt (IHB) 7601 for a time..then to GE.
4. Went to GE as a leaser, then to Larrys truck & electric, then scrapped by Larry.
5. sold and went to Mexico in 2003.
6. Will become IHB 7601 *after* rebuilding in Mexico!

I have seen no photo evidence of an actual IHB 7601.

She is likely scrapped by now..but with all the conflicting info, its hard to know for sure!
The most likely scenerio is that after being sold by CP, she was a GE leaser, sold to Larry, then scrapped by Larry.
but the "sold to Mexico" story adds in an interesting twist..she wouldnt be sold to Mexico (presumably by Larry), AND also scrapped by Larry!
you cant have both..
So im giving it 90% odds "scrapped at Larry's truck and electric"
and 10% odds she still exists in Mexico..can anyone really confirm either way?

*note 2
Erie GP7 1207.
rebuilt to GP8 by Conrail @Silvis 1978
CMGN 9210 leaves town...
Paul Cameron reports that Central Michigan Railway has sold GP-8 #9210 and it is heading south today (3/7/2003).
9210 left Bay City for Durand this morning to be forwarded to the CN.
It has been reportedly sold to an elevator in southern Illinois.
This leaves its sibling 9209 out of service at North Bay City with major generator problems.
9209 is also looking for a buyer.  These two GP-8's on the CMGN lasted just over ten years, having entered service later in 1992.
from: http://www.michiganrailroads.com/MichRRs/News/2003Briefs/March2003.htm

                                            9209 is - current CB&G 9209 - ex CMGM 9209 - ex CR 5440 - ex CR 5756 - ex PC 5786 - nee NYC 5786

*note 11
Concerning EL 423 (BVRY 8201, 2nd) an EMD NW2.
The unit was operating with BVRY, but I recieved an email from Greg Kulp who said the unit has been stripped down,
and is being converted to a "Captive Unit"
Greg says: "A captive loco is similar to a slug.  It draws power from a 2 conductor 250 VDC catenary, is radio controlled,
and has a compressor for its air brakes.  Four old ALCO S2's are currently in such service, due to be replaced with the above NW2's plus 2 other EMD's.
At least a little good news, some components and sheet metal from both above NW2's have been (or will be)
donated to the Reading Co. Technical & Historical Society for use in their NW2 (RDG 103)."
January 2013
thanks Greg!

Update, December 2012.

Another EL loco has been lost, one of the big SD45-2's.
EL 3680 was scrapped at Larry's Truck & Electric in McDonald, OH, in December 2011.
EL 3680 went to Conrail 6665 in 1976, then to CSX 8976 in the 1999 split.
CSX retired the locomotive in 2005, and in November 2005 it became Ohio Central 8976.
The unit sat stored on the OC for several years, until the Genesee & Wyoming bought the OC in 2008.
3680 didnt "make the cut" (it might not have been operable) and the unit was sold to Larrys Truck & Electric,
where it was scrapped in December 2011.
