if you want more details, just send me an email.

Where you see a green number in parentheses, like this:
(9) Mary Esther (ACKLES) LAWRENCE.
the person in question is a direct ancestor of mine, and the number refers to their position
on my main pedigree chart, where I am person number 1, my parents are 2 and 3, etc..

Also, this page will follow the same "color convention" I am using elsewhere on this site.
People in Green text are my direct ancestors.
People in Grey text are relatives, (cousins, aunts, uncles, etc.) but not my direct ancestors.
or records that *might* be about relatives of mine, but I have not yet been able to confirm a relationship.

This record contains a mystery for me..
It lists my Great Great Great Grandfather's name as (32) "David Campbell Lawrence"
(father of (16) James Campbell Lawrence, who's name is not in question.)
This is the only record I have seen for a David Campbell Lawrence, and I believe this information is incorrect.
Because the records of Prudence Fosdick Lawrence, (source 11 above)  list a Jacob Lawrence, born 18 NOV 1817,
with a son James from Waverly, NY..so based on that, I believe Jacob is actually my (32) Great Great Great Grandfather. Also, my source #7, the pedigree chart drawn by my Grandfather's brother, shows Jacob Lawrence was married to (33) Margaret Ann DEGROFF, and my Dad knows that Degroff's were relatives of ours, and several Degroff's are buried in Glenwood cemetary alongside several Lawrence's..So im quite certain Jacob Lawrence is my ancestor, not David Campbell Lawrence. I dont know who David Campbell Lawrence is, or why his name is associated with my actual ancestor (16) James Campbell Lawrence, I will try to learn more.

I have read in genealogy books "NEVER write on old photographs!"
I dont agree with that! because I am very grateful my grandfather had the foresight to write, in light pencil, on the back of all these photos, with the names of the people in the photos!
THANK YOU Grandfather! If not for his writing, we wouldnt know who all these people are. Some genealogists may cringe, but im in favor of writing on photos! (using pencil only, and writing lightly on the back.)

I also have a much older  family photo album, that came along with all my grandfather's photos, with photos of people I can only assume are old relatives and ancestors..some of the photos are tin-types! all clearly 19th century images..but none are marked..apparently they are so old even my Grandfather didnt know who they were. Now, their identities are lost forever..there is no one alive who could possibly identify them.

I believe they might all be Ackles, (my Grandfather's Mother's family) because many of the photos have studio names from Spencer, NY on the back.
I wish someone had written on the back of them too, like my Grandfather did with the newer photos. Here is one of the mystery photos:

 Baptism certificate of my father, (2) Richard Lee Lawrence.
This is very interesting!
Im going through my Dad's papers in October 2009, a few months after he passed away, and I come across his Baptism Certificate, I have never seen it before..
My Dad was baptised April 4, 1942, in Waverly, NY. When he was 7 months old.

The baptism certificate itself isnt surprising..what IS surprising is that it it seems to be a CATHOLIC baptism certificate! I have never heard of any Lawrence's being Catholic! As far as I have always known I come from a Christian heritage, but definately not Catholic specifically..

I do want to explore the religious beliefs of my ancestors, and post anything I find out about their religious lives..churches they went to, denominations, different religions in my family tree, etc..this adds an interesting new twist!

Update!  further research shows the wording and artwork might not be Catholic-specific.
it could also be Lutheran or some other denomination..stay tuned!

Update 2!
I posted a scan of the certificate (minus names and dates) here.
turns out its an Episcopal church..
Grace Church in Waverly to be exact. 441 Park ave, Waverly, NY
That is where my Dad was baptised in 1942.
(Thank you Kate for the info!)

Even before I found this out, I have been intrigued by the Episcopalians! there is a very interesting Episcopalian church on East Ave in Rochester, across from the George Eastman House..My wife and I were walking around the neighborhood a few years ago, commenting on the church, saying it "looks Catholic" (im very impressed by Catholic artwork! they really know how to decorate a church!) when I first thought "Who are the Episcopalians?? how do they fit into the whole "Christian" family of denominations?"  We didnt know..so I googled them!

It turns out the Episcopalians are essentially "Catholics twice removed from Rome!"
First there was the Catholic Church..
Then, in 1534, King  Henry VIII of England broke ties with Rome and the Pope
to make himself the head of the Church of England.
Then!  in 1776, we had English citizens (and members of the Church of England) in America who suddenly found themselves no longer tied to England, because of the American Revolution!
they clearly could no longer be members of the Church of England..what to do?
The Episcopal church was created..basically Catholics who seperated from Rome in 1534,
then seperated from England in 1776..the Episcopalians have retained much of the original Catholic heritage and rites, and they consider themselves "Protestant, yet Catholic"

obviously thats all a huge simplification! ;)
but thats the basic story as I understand it..(if any of that is wrong, please let me know!)

So its very interesting to me to find out that I have Episcopal ancestors..
I still dont know if the Episcopal heritage is from my Lawrence side, or my Grandmother's HILL side!

My Late 20th Century Lawrence's were not church-goers..I dont know why exactly, just one of those things..although I have been re-exploring my Christian heritage the last few years, since about age 35.. My wife and I went to North Baptist Church for a few years, (We only left North because we moved.), and now we attend Pearce Memorial Church. Pearce happens to be Free Methodist, but I dont consider myself to belong to any specific Protestant denomination..Im a Christian first and foremost, the exact demoniation isnt terribly important to me right now.. Im very intersted in the religious lives of my ancestors! eventually I might add a new page to this website collection just to share what I have discovered about their various religious beliefs! since that is also a big part of my personal heritage.
