* note 8
Concerning the heritage, and the previous numbers, of LAL C424's Number 421, 422, 423, and 424.
There has been some confusion about the previous numbers of these four units, and it's all Guilfords fault! ;)
Guilford renumbured two of the units into Springfied Terminal 70-series numbers, and two others got ST numbers "on paper only"..
Then they were given D&H numbers again, then they got a second (and final) set of Guilford numbers! This time being
lettered for Maine Central instead of Springfield Terminal..The MEC numbers went back to the D&H 450 series,
but not in the same sequence as before! The MEC numbers are the numbers the units wore when they came to the LAL in 1996.

LAL 421 was only 452.
LAL 422  was 451 and  455.
LAL 423 was only 454.
LAL 424 was 450 and 451.

Also, LAL 421 and 423 recieved Springfield Terminal numbers 71 and 73 *on paper only*..They never phyically wore those numbers.
So when they "went back" to the D&H, they were still wearing their original numbers.

However the other two units, B&H 422 and LAL 424, *did* in fact wear ST numbers 74 and 70, so when they recieved their MEC
numbers, they got different numbers than the original D&H numbers..

Also, there has been some conflicting information on the ST numbers some of the units wore "on paper only"..
Historical data, from the Leatherstocking chapter NRHS, from the 1987 timeframe (the time the C424's were given the ST numbers)
supports the idea that the ST numbers were assigned in chronological order..see the link below for the discussion on the forum.
This is believed to be the correct sequence:

D&H 451 - ST 70 - (Received Guilford paint, and lettered as ST 70)
D&H 452 - ST 71 - (Was ST 71 on paper only.)
D&H 453 - ST 72 - (Was ST 72 on paper only.)
D&H 454 - ST 73 - (Was ST 73 on paper only.)
D&H 455 - ST 74 - (Received Guilford paint, and lettered as ST 74)
D&H 456 - ST 75 - (Received Guilford paint, and lettered as ST 75)

This was happening in 1987..two years later, in 1989, ex-B&M EMD GP9's got numbers ST 71 and ST 72.

This has all led to a TON of incorrect information on the internet about these units! wrong numbers are posted all over.
Based on reliable research, I believe the number sequence on this page is now correct.
